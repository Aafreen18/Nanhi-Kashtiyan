our programs

Educating the Children

Nanhi Kashtiyan have been preparing kids for schooling, an effective in-house preparatory curriculum is practiced. It helps children to learn & develop on their pace, as per their age and learning levels. It inspires joyful learning in an engaging environment

More than 600 children across all projects attended the programme with Nanhi Kashtiyan. 276 children joined formal schools after completing the programme. The progress of children is tracked every months and it was observed that all of them were settling in well with promising academic performance.

Bridging learning level gaps

Children to Nanhi Kashtiyan come from diverse and complex social and emotional histories. Consistent investments in building their capacities and academic skills require continuous efforts. Under a new initiative children needing additional attention were identified from grade I to VIII including those appearing for Board Exams. Adopting NCERT guidelines for age-appropriate learning levels under the National curriculum framework, structured attention has been extended to these children. The assessments show significant development in learning levels.

Emergency Response

Nanhi Kashtiyan has always moved deftly to answer the call of the children in need in many natural and man-made disasters. Whether it is pandemic of Covid 19, Earthquakes in Uttarkashi & Nepal. all these tragedies involved massive destruction and deaths.

Children have been the worst sufferers of these events. While many lost their homes, a large bunch of children lost their families, too. At all these places, Nanhi Kashtiyan has undertaken emergency relief work and rehabilitation programmes; we also established various live kitchens, dry rations medicines and other relief kits based on identified needs

Women empowerment

Women in India have to endure many ordeals in their day-to-day lives. For centuries, women of all generations have had to struggle to be treated as equals. At present, thousands of women in India are marginalized and poorly treated within society resulting in a lack of the skills and confidence needed to live fulfilled and active lives. Currently, a staggering 245 million women across India are unable to read or write, this severely limits their position and opportunities available to them within the society. Nanhi Kashtiyan for women empowerment, stepped in to create an impact by changing the lives of women from these backgrounds, by creating the set goals and training sessions to make them self-sustainable in life.

Women Health & Personal hygiene

At least 500 million women and girls globally lack adequate facilities for menstrual hygiene management. The challenge menstruating girls and women face is often less tangible than simply the availability of infrastructure, and is rooted in social norms and awareness. In many cultures, menstruating women are considered impure and are systematically excluded from participating in every-day activities, such as education, employment, and cultural and religious practices. Moreover, the taboos and stigmas attached to menstruation lead to an overall culture of silence around the topic, resulting in limited information on menstruation and menstrual hygiene. Such misinformation can have ramifications on the health and dignity of girls and women.
